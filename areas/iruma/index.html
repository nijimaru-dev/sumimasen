Iruma — Off the Tourist Map

30 minutes from Shinjuku on the Seibu Ikebukuro Line. Zero tourist buses, no selfie sticks. Japan's largest premium outlet mall — and the local ramen that visitors miss entirely.

Getting here: Seibu Ikebukuro Line from Ikebukuro Station → Iruma-shi Station (特急 Limited Express, ~30 min, ¥780). A free shuttle bus runs to the Outlets from the station on weekends.

✓ English staff

Mitsui Outlet Park Iruma

Premium outlet mall · 340+ stores · All budgets

💡 Japan's largest outlet mall — Nike, Adidas, Coach, Ralph Lauren, and many Japanese brands at 30–70% off retail. Staff at major brand stores speak English. Tax refund counter on-site (passport required). Bring a big bag. Open 10 am–8 pm; food court until 9 pm.

✓ Photo menu

Station area ramen & soba

Local dining near Iruma-shi Station · ¥700–1,200

💡 The cluster of small restaurants around Iruma-shi Station is where locals eat — not the outlet food court. Ramen shops in this area have photo menus; point at what looks good. Prices are 20–30% less than Tokyo for the same quality. Open from 11 am, many close by 8 pm.

✓ Tourist-friendly

Sayama Tea Fields (狭山茶)

Green tea farms & shops · Spring harvest (April–May) is peak

💡 Iruma is the center of Sayama tea production — one of Japan's three great green teas. Local tea shops sell loose-leaf tea and matcha sweets. Less known than Uji (Kyoto) tea but equally serious quality. Some farms offer English-guided experiences in spring.
