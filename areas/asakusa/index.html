Asakusa — English-Friendly Picks

Old Tokyo's most intact neighborhood. Senso-ji draws 30 million visitors a year — the area is well-practiced at dealing with people who don't speak Japanese.

Getting here: Asakusa Station on the Tokyo Metro Ginza Line or Toei Asakusa Line. From Shinjuku: ~25 min. From Tokyo Station: ~20 min.

✓ Tourist-friendly

Nakamise-dori (仲見世通り)

Traditional snacks & souvenirs · All budgets

💡 The 89-stall shopping street leading to Senso-ji. Every vendor is used to foreign visitors — pointing works fine. Cash recommended; some stalls are cash-only. Ningyo-yaki (small sponge cakes) from any stall are ¥200–350 and excellent.

✓ English-friendly

Kappabashi Kitchen Street (かっぱ橋道具街)

Cooking equipment, plastic food models · 10 min walk from Senso-ji

💡 The street where restaurants buy their equipment and those perfect plastic food display models. Staff are used to foreign buyers. A plastic sushi set (¥800–2,000) makes a better souvenir than anything at the airport. Closed Sundays.

✓ English menu available

Hoppy Street (ホッピー通り)

Izakaya street · ¥500–1,500/dish

💡 A narrow street of old-school izakaya (Japanese gastropubs). Hoppy is a non-alcoholic beer-flavored drink mixed with shochu — the local specialty. Most stalls have picture menus. Order Hoppy + motsu (beef offal stew) for the authentic Asakusa experience. Open from around noon.

✓ English menu

Mugitoro (麦とろ)

Traditional Japanese · ¥3,000–5,000

💡 Known for tororo (grated mountain yam) over rice — a traditional Tokyo dish you won't find easily elsewhere. English menu available. Reservations recommended for dinner. The building overlooks the Sumida River.
