Shibuya — English-Friendly Picks

The most photographed intersection on earth is also one of Tokyo's most foreigner-dense neighborhoods. Staff in this area are used to non-Japanese speakers.

Getting here: Shibuya Station — served by JR (Yamanote, Shonan-Shinjuku lines), Tokyo Metro (Ginza, Hanzomon, Fukutoshin lines), and Tokyu lines. From Shinjuku: 3 min. From Tokyo Station: 25 min.

✓ Tourist landmark

Shibuya Scramble Crossing

The crossing · Best viewed from Mag's Park or Starbucks above

💡 3,000 people cross simultaneously when the lights change. Best view: Shibuya Sky (top of Scramble Square, ¥2,000 entry) or the free window seat at Starbucks in the Tsutaya building. Rush hour (8–9 am, 6–7 pm) is the most intense.

✓ English menus available

Shibuya Hikarie (渋谷ヒカリエ)

Department store with restaurant floors · All budgets

💡 Connected directly to Shibuya Station. The ShinQs food floors (B2–B3) have high-quality takeaway food and sweets. Restaurant floors (6–7F) have picture menus and some English menus. Good option when the streets feel overwhelming — air-conditioned, calm, quality food.

✓ English ordering system

Ichiran Ramen (一蘭)

Ramen · ¥980–1,500 · Solo dining designed

💡 The solo-booth ramen chain where you order via a form (English available) and eat in your own curtained booth. Zero social interaction required — ideal for jet-lagged arrivals or introverts. Multiple Shibuya locations; ticket machine at entry.

✓ Tourist-friendly

Takeshita Street, Harajuku (竹下通り)

Street food & fashion · 10 min walk from Shibuya · ¥300–800 snacks

💡 The pedestrian street famous for cosplay fashion and rainbow-colored crepes. Vendors sell cotton candy, crepes, and bubble tea. All street food vendors are used to tourists. Best on weekday mornings before the crowds arrive. Harajuku Station (one stop on the Yamanote Line) is right at the entrance.
