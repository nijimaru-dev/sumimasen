20 Japanese Foods to Try (With Ordering Phrases)

Restaurants · 6 min read · By Nijimaru · June 2026
This article contains no affiliate links. About our disclosures →

Ordering food you can't identify is one of the best parts of visiting Japan. This guide covers 20 dishes worth trying — what they are, how to order them, and what to watch for (allergies, surprises, etiquette). Prices are Tokyo/Osaka estimates for 2026.

これをください I'll have this one (while pointing) Kore wo kudasai Copy →
ラーメン Ramen Raamen

Wheat noodles in broth — tonkotsu (pork bone), shoyu (soy), miso, or shio (salt). Regional variations are significant. Order at the ticket machine in most shops.

¥800–1,400 / ~$5–9
寿司 Sushi Sushi

Raw fish on rice at high-end places; at kaiten (conveyor belt) restaurants, grab plates from the belt or order via touchscreen. Kaiten sushi is ¥100–600 per plate.

¥150–800/plate (kaiten) · ¥5,000+ (omakase)
天ぷら Tempura Tenpura

Lightly battered and fried seafood and vegetables. Dip in the light dashi broth provided. Often served over rice (tendon) or with soba noodles.

¥1,000–2,500 / ~$7–17
焼き鳥 Yakitori Yakitori

Grilled chicken skewers. Order "tare" (sweet sauce) or "shio" (salt). The izakaya setting is informal — order several skewers with beer.

¥150–350/skewer ⚠ Some parts are offal — ask if uncertain
おにぎり Onigiri Onigiri

Rice balls wrapped in seaweed. Filled with tuna mayo (ツナマヨ), salmon (さけ), pickled plum (梅), or other options. Available at every konbini, 24 hours.

¥130–180 / ~$0.85–1.20
うどん Udon Udon

Thick wheat noodles in dashi broth. Kake udon is the basic version. Kakiage (vegetable tempura on top) is a common add-on. Often ordered at a food ticket machine.

¥400–900 / ~$2.60–6
そば Soba Soba

Buckwheat noodles, served hot or cold. Zaru soba (cold, with dipping sauce) is the summer classic. The broth is dashi-based — noticeably different from Western broths.

¥600–1,200 / ~$4–8 ⚠ Contains gluten (buckwheat flour often mixed with wheat)
たこ焼き Takoyaki Takoyaki

Osaka's signature street food: round balls of dough filled with octopus, topped with mayo and bonito flakes. Served very hot — wait 90 seconds before biting.

¥500–800 (6–8 pieces) ⚠ Extremely hot inside — do not bite immediately
お好み焼き Okonomiyaki Okonomiyaki

Savory pancake with cabbage, egg, meat or seafood. Often cooked at the table on a built-in griddle. The name means "grilled as you like it."

¥800–1,500 / ~$5–10
カレーライス Curry Rice Karee raisu

Japanese curry is thicker and milder than Indian curry, served over rice with fukujinzuke (pickled vegetables). Common at CoCo Ichibanya chain — order spice level 1–10.

¥700–1,200 / ~$5–8
とんかつ Tonkatsu Tonkatsu

Breaded and deep-fried pork cutlet served with shredded cabbage and tonkatsu sauce. You usually grind your own sesame seeds into the sauce at the table.

¥1,200–2,000 / ~$8–13
餃子 Gyoza Gyoza

Pan-fried pork and cabbage dumplings. Mix soy sauce, rice vinegar, and chili oil from the table bottles for the dipping sauce. Often ordered as a side to ramen.

¥300–600 (6 pieces)
しゃぶしゃぶ Shabu-Shabu Shabu-shabu

Hot pot — swish thin-sliced meat in boiling dashi broth, then dip in sesame or ponzu sauce. Interactive dining; good for groups.

¥2,000–4,000 / ~$13–27
焼肉 Yakiniku Yakiniku

Korean-influenced BBQ — grill marinated meat yourself on a tabletop grill. Order by the cut. Karubi (short rib) and rosu (sirloin) are approachable starting points.

¥2,000–5,000 / ~$13–33
刺身 Sashimi Sashimi

Raw fish without rice, served with wasabi and soy sauce. Mix the wasabi into the soy sauce sparingly — Japanese wasabi is genuinely hot.

¥800–2,000 / ~$5–13 ⚠ Raw fish — consult a doctor if immunocompromised
茶碗蒸し Chawanmushi Chawanmushi

Savory egg custard steamed in a cup with shrimp, chicken, and mitsuba herb inside. Subtly flavored, served hot. Common at traditional restaurants and izakaya.

¥300–700 / ~$2–5
もつ鍋 Motsu Nabe Motsu nabe

Offal hot pot — beef or pork intestines with cabbage and garlic chive in miso or soy broth. A Fukuoka specialty, increasingly common in Tokyo. Not for everyone; genuinely good if it is for you.

¥1,500–2,500 / ~$10–17 ⚠ Contains offal
抹茶スイーツ Matcha sweets Maccha suiitsu

Matcha (powdered green tea) flavors ice cream, mochi, cake, latte. More bitter and less sweet than Western chocolate. The real version is notably different from fake matcha flavor products outside Japan.

¥300–800 / ~$2–5
たい焼き Taiyaki Taiyaki

Fish-shaped waffle filled with red bean paste (anko) or custard. Sold at street stalls and small shops. Eat warm. The tail is stuffed too — don't leave it.

¥150–250 / ~$1–1.65
日本酒 Sake (nihonshu) Nihonshu

Rice wine, served hot or cold. Ask for "hiya" (cold) or "atsukan" (hot). Junmai daiginjo is the premium grade; futsushu is table sake. Pairs well with sashimi and yakitori.

¥400–800/glass

Written by Nijimaru — a nurse living in Japan. Prices are Tokyo/Osaka 2026 estimates. Menus and prices change — treat these as order-of-magnitude guides, not quotes.
