About

Written by someone who actually lives here.

Not a travel agency. Not AI-generated. A working nurse in Japan who got tired of watching tourists struggle with things that shouldn't be hard.

Why this site exists

Every week I watch visitors at train stations — confused, a little embarrassed, standing in front of a ticket machine they can't read while the line builds behind them. The information to fix this exists, but it's buried in Reddit threads, Facebook groups, and guidebooks written by people who haven't been to Japan in five years.

I started Sumimasen to put that knowledge somewhere permanent and actually usable — something you can open in the moment, not just before you leave home.

What makes this different

  • Written from daily life in Japan, not a two-week trip
  • Honest about what's awkward, not just what's polite
  • Built to work offline — phrases load without cell service
  • No filler content — every article solves a specific problem
  • Medical-profession attention to accuracy (I don't guess)

Editorial standards

I write from experience and verify against official sources. When I'm not certain, I say so. Prices are shown in both ¥ and $ (USD equivalent). Distances in both km and miles. Nothing on this site is AI-generated — every word is written and reviewed by a human who lives here.

When I recommend a product (JR Pass, eSIM, etc.), it's because I've used it or compared it carefully. Some links are affiliate links — I earn a small commission at no cost to you. Full disclosure is on the Affiliate Disclosure page.

Who I am

I'm a registered nurse currently working in Japan, operating this site under the name Nijimaru (虹丸). I keep my personal and professional identities separate — this is a side project, not affiliated with my employer in any way.

I'm not a Japan expert by title. I'm someone who has navigated these situations every day for years and wants to make your trip a little less stressful.
