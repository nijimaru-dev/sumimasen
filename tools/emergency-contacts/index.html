Emergency Contacts in Japan

Official sources only. All links go directly to government or institutional websites — not reproduced here, so the information is always current.

Important: This page links to official sources rather than listing phone numbers directly. Emergency numbers and procedures change — official government pages are always up to date. Save this page to your home screen before you travel.

Police

Embassies & consulates

For lost passports, serious legal trouble, or situations where you need your government's assistance. Contact your embassy — not the host country's police — if you're detained.

Emergency citizen services, lost passports, arrest assistance. Locations in Tokyo, Osaka, Sapporo, Fukuoka, Naha.

Travel insurance & medical

💳

Travel insurance

Call the number on the back of your travel insurance card first. Most US credit cards (Chase Sapphire, Amex Platinum) include travel insurance — check before you go.

Keep your policy number in your phone's notes app, not just the physical card.

💊

Medication in Japan

Some US medications are restricted in Japan (certain ADHD medications, pseudoephedrine-based cold medicines). Check the Ministry of Health's list before traveling.
